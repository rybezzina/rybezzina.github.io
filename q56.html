Question 5


Describe how you would test your website to make sure it looks as intended. What kind of files have you used in your website and explain why you have chosen those filetypes. How can you test your website to ensure it is accessible to people with disabilities? Describe how you can overcome potential accessibility issues with your website



As I used Trello to plan my website out, I tried to stick to it as much as I could. This meant that there were some things that I had to push back because they were too complicated at the time or I just simply did not get the time to get round to them. As I went along with my website, my family would come in and take a look at what I was up to. Although at first it was a little frustrating, I decided to take advantage of that and ask them for suggestions on what I could add or change to the website. I added their suggestions to Trello and looked back and forth to improve my website.

Using their suggestions, I decided to keep the size of the text at a larger size than other websites, as some people may struggle to read the smaller letters. The larder letters are much more clear to read than a smaller sized font, and using arial also made it very readable. I also think that adding the boxes around the text made it easier to read and follow for people that have a harder time concentrating.

To make sure that my website looked as intended, I kept saving the code after writing something extra and refreshing my web page. This would give me a live update of what I was changing.

I have used a CSS file on my website which contains all of the styling methods. This was a very good thing to do as it was much simpler to go back and change something, and it also applied to multiple things throughout my other files. This meant that I could make all of the text boxes like this one have a border around them with one line of code, instead of making it add a box every single time I added text.



Question 6


Describe ways to improve your productivity and efficiency throughout this project. If you could have been more productive or efficient, how would you implement this for a future project?



To improve my productivity and efficiency on this project, I think I could have had a tab open for a website like Stack overflow. Stack overflow is essentially a message/help board online that people can ask questions and give answers on. It would have helped with my productivity and efficiency as I would have had a quicker answer to the problems I ran in to, such as resizing and positioning my images. Although help is eventually found, there is a chance that someone may have given an answer that you dont understand and it might send you into confusion even more than you already were. To make sure I am more efficient in a future project, I will make sure to open up a board such as Stack overflow, or find something similar so that I can have answers faster than what I had them this time around.
